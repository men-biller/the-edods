Cucumber Forum's EDODS

Welcome to Cucumber Forum’s EDODS (Everyday-Object Drum Sequencer).

All the percussive sounds in the sequencer are sourced from common household objects. Some of the sounds featured include pots and pans, a dresser being kicked, and a ceramic bowl.

The tiles represent “steps” in the drum loop, indicating whether that part of the sequence activates the associated sound. They can be toggled on or off to create the desired rhythm or beat. The tempo slider adjusts the speed of the sequencer, and the reset button clears the grid entirely.

Above each track, a gear icon opens an effects panel that controls reverb, delay, and the wet/dry mix for each sound. To reset the effects, refresh the page.

What sets this sequencer apart is its unpredictability: not only are the tracks unlabeled, but they are randomly rearranged every time the page is loaded or refreshed. The only way to learn how to use this sequencer—and other virtual drum machines—is to experiment, even if it doesn’t sound perfect on the first few tries.

There’s no manual and no right or wrong way to use it, which is what makes it fun.

So, what are you waiting for? Get started!
